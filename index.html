Everyday Verbs & Conjugation

Past-tense verb conjugations, useful expressions, and key vocabulary from daily life

Past-Tense Pronoun Endings (اللواحق)

I (أنا) -it
You m. (أنت) -it
You f. (أنتِ) -ti
He (هو) — (no ending)
She (هي) -at
We (نحنا) -na
You pl. (أنتو)-tu
They (هُما) -u
Note: For hollow verbs (verbs with و or ي in the root like زار، نام، باع، قام), the vowel in the stem changes when endings are added. Watch the tables below!

Quick Practice (تدرّب)

١. How do you say "She visited" in Jordanian dialect?

✅ زارت – hiya zārat. The hollow verb keeps its long vowel for هي.

٢. Which ending do you add to a verb for "We" (نحنا)?

✅ -na is the ending for نحنا. Example: كتبنا (katabna) – we wrote.

٣. What does بلّش (bellash) mean?

✅ بلّش means "to start / begin". Very common in Jordanian dialect!

٤. "هون" means:

✅ هون = here. هناك = there.

٥. Conjugate حكى for "I" (أنا):

✅ حكيت (hakeit) – I talked/told.
